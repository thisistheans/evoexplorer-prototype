Case study 1

Predict: Population B

At the end of the Industrial Revolution, the moth population consisted of approximately 50% dark peppered moths.

If colour is non-heritable, which colour would you see most of in the moth population after a few decades of reduced population (light-coloured tree barks)?

  • There’s an increase in light peppered moths and decrease in dark peppered moths because moths need to camouflage and avoid predation.
  • There’s an increase in light peppered moths and decrease in dark peppered moths because light peppered moths are predated upon less and are more likely to survive and reproduce.
  • There are no significant changes in the population because the traits are not passed down so cannot be selected for.
  • There are no significant changes in the population because the original population consisted of approximately equal number of light to dark peppered moths.
